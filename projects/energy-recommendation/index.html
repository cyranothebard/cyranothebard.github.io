Energy Recommendation System

An intelligent recommendation system for sustainable energy decisions, powered by modern ML.

Executive Summary

Built a modular, production-ready recommendation system that forecasts short-term demand and coordinates building-level reductions to support grid stability. The system processes 8,000+ buildings in under 30 seconds, maintains a validated 5.4% grid reduction capability (within 2–7% industry benchmarks), and enables parallel team development through standardized interfaces.

Problem Statement & Business Context

Utilities face rising volatility in demand patterns. Blackout prevention and demand coordination at city scale can deliver $2–5M in annual value via avoided outages and deferred infrastructure spend. We designed a three-stage ML pipeline—feature engineering, compliance prediction, and portfolio optimization—to deliver actionable, policy-aware recommendations aligned with grid operations.

Results & Performance Analysis

Validated system performance meets operational and business targets. The pipeline processes 8,000+ buildings in under 30 seconds and sustains ≥99.7% uptime with ≤200 ms P95 latency for recommendation retrieval.

  • Forecast quality: Temporal validation prevents leakage; cohort-level error analysis confirms stable performance across 15 building types.
  • Grid impact: Achieves a 5.4% coordinated reduction, aligned with 2–7% industry benchmarks for demand response.
  • Scalability: Modular design enables distributed execution paths and parallel team development without integration friction.
  • Reliability: Input validation and monitoring guard against delayed/missing weather data and model drift.

These results translate to meaningful utility value via avoided outages and deferred infrastructure spend, while the architecture supports rapid iteration and safe deployment.
